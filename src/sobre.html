About Studio Ghibli

Studio Ghibli was founded in 1985 by Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata, Toshio Suzuki and Yasuyoshi Tokuma, right after the success of Kaze no Tani no Nausicaä in the previous year and soon after the purchase of the Topcraft studio. The studio released its first film, Tenkū no Shiro Rapyuta, the following year. The company logo is Totoro, a character from the film Tonari no Totoro, released in 1988. With the exception of six productions, all films in the studio were headed by Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata. Toshio Suzuki, in turn, is the producer of most from them. In 2001, the Ghibli Museum, a museum dedicated to the studio's works, opened.
